Travel Tips for Sabah

Weather

Sabah enjoys a tropical climate year-round, with warm and humid conditions typical of Borneo. Daytime temperatures range from 23°C to 32°C, and nights are generally cooler in the highlands. The wet season lasts from November to March, bringing heavy rainfall especially along the coast and in rural areas. If you’re planning jungle treks, island trips, or mountain climbs, it’s best to schedule them during the drier months of April to October. Don’t forget sunscreen, a hat, and a light raincoat just in case of sudden showers!

Transportation

Getting around Sabah is quite accessible, though some areas require planning. In Kota Kinabalu, you can move around using taxis, buses, or e-hailing apps like Grab. For more remote destinations or nature parks, renting a car gives you flexibility and convenience. Minivans and shared taxis serve smaller towns but may not follow strict schedules. To explore the offshore islands, ferries and speedboats depart from Jesselton Point Ferry Terminal. Always confirm fares in advance, especially with independent taxi drivers, to avoid confusion.

Safety Tips

Sabah is known for being safe and welcoming, but like any travel destination, taking precautions is important. Keep valuables like passports and cash secured, especially in crowded areas. Avoid isolated spots after dark and stick to well-lit areas when walking. If you're going for hikes, diving, or wildlife adventures, always go with a certified guide and follow local advice. Check weather and sea conditions before heading out to the islands. A little caution goes a long way in ensuring a smooth and enjoyable trip.
