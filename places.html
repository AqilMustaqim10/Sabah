Top Tourist Attractions in Sabah

Kinabalu Park

Kinabalu Park is Malaysia’s very first UNESCO World Heritage Site and a crown jewel of Sabah. Dominated by the towering Mount Kinabalu, the highest peak in Southeast Asia, the park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. It boasts over 5,000 plant species, rare orchids, exotic birds, and cool mountain air that offers a refreshing escape from city life. Whether you're a casual hiker or a serious climber, the park’s trails offer something for everyone amid breathtaking scenery.

Sipadan Island

Widely regarded as one of the top dive sites in the world, Sipadan Island is a diver’s dream. This oceanic island, formed by living corals growing on an extinct volcanic cone, is teeming with marine life. Expect encounters with sea turtles, reef sharks, barracudas, and swirling schools of fish. The waters are crystal clear, the reefs are vibrant, and the biodiversity is unmatched. Strict visitor limits help preserve the ecosystem, making every visit a rare and special experience.

Lok Kawi Wildlife Park

Just a short drive from Kota Kinabalu, Lok Kawi Wildlife Park offers a glimpse into Borneo’s unique and endangered wildlife. Designed with families in mind, the park features a zoological section and a botanical garden. You’ll find orangutans, proboscis monkeys, Malayan tigers, and pygmy elephants in spacious enclosures that aim to mimic their natural habitats. It’s a great stop for learning about Sabah’s wildlife conservation efforts and for enjoying a fun, educational outing.
