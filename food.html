Latok

Latok is a traditional Sabah delicacy made from seaweed, often served fresh as a salad. It has a crunchy texture with a salty and slightly tangy taste, typically enjoyed with sambal chili and a squeeze of lime. Latok is popular along the coastal regions of Sabah and is prized for its refreshing flavor and natural goodness.

Tuaran Fried Noodles

Tuaran Fried Noodles (Mee Tuaran) is a famous noodle dish from Tuaran town in Sabah. Known for its springy texture and smoky wok hei flavor, these flat egg noodles are stir-fried with pork slices, vegetables, and soy sauce. It’s a beloved comfort food that combines savory, aromatic, and slightly crispy elements.

Ambuyat

Ambuyat is a starchy dish made from sago flour and water, forming a sticky, glue-like texture. Commonly eaten with a bamboo fork called "chandas," it’s usually dipped into a variety of flavorful side dishes, sambals, and curries. Ambuyat is not just food — it’s a shared cultural experience, especially popular among the Bruneian Malay and indigenous communities in Sabah.
